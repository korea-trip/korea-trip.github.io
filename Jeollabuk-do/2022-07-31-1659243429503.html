Baraebong Peak (바래봉)

Jeollabuk-doNatureNatural SitesMountains

Overview

Baraebong Peak gets its name from its resemblance to an upside down <em>barittae</em> (a wooden rice bowl used by temple priests). The mountain is characterized by round slopes and fields of royal azalea flowers in spring. Trekking starts at Yongsan Village, 1.5 kilometers away from Unbong-eup. The trail follows a gentle path through a forest reserve behind a ranch and up to the peak. The last section of the trail before reaching the summit is covered in royal azaleas in full bloom from late April until May. The view from the peak includes the whole vista of Jirisan Mountain.
