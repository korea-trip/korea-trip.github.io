Hapcheon Haeinsa Temple (해인사 (합천))

Gyeongsangnam-doCulture/Art/HistoryHistorical SitesTemples

Overview

Haeinsa Temple is one of Koreas three largest temples, and was founded during the third year of King Ae-jangs reign (802) by two monks Suneung and Ijung. The name Haein originates from the expression Haeinsammae of Hwaeomgyeong (Buddhist scripture), which means truly enlightened world of Buddha and our naturally undefiled mind. Along with the Printing Woodblocks of the Tripitaka Koreana (National Treasure No. 32) and Janggyeongpanjeon Depositories (National Treasure No. 52), various treasures are enshrined at Haeinsa Temple.
